Generative Studies Local Rules / Emergent Form

VISION&SOUND

01Pattern How does a flock of birds swivel so gracefully around the modern city skyline?

How do two flowers, on mountains across corners of the planet, grow to evolve similar geometries?

02Rule 50 years ago, John Conway conceived a discrete grid model of computation where complex, emergent behaviors arise from incredibly simple local rules.

The mesmerizing diversity of the natural world, our biology, and our societies, can be interpreted through finite local rules.

03Motivation While most generative artificial intelligence today relies on regurgitation and remixing of spoon-fed data, I believe we can generate a beautiful landscape from capturing token concepts as simple rules and letting them self-evolve.

04Instrument I explore local interactions as a generative source of beauty in vision and sound.

Can core sounds self-interact to generate a sonic landscape as graceful to the human ear, as a flock of birds is to the human eye?

Art Installation

Touch-Guided Self-Evolving Cellular Automata Display

2026 Work In Progress
Estimated Time Input: 85 Physical Hours Running Cost: $445 USD

This art piece aims to bring John Conway’s seminal Game of Life to LIFE. I treat physical touch as a capacitative source of life: a hand enters the field, a cell wakes, and the grid begins to compose itself based on algorithmic rules.

Simulate the Game of Life: create forms, see them evolve, sustain, replicate, or die.
Live Cells: 0
Space: Pause/Resume the simulation. C: Clear the grid. V: Toggle the Void Seed rule when grid has fewer than 3 cells. Left Click: Draw/activate cells. Right Click: Erase cells.

A new premise for science: simple rules can create infinite complexity

Interactive Performance & Sound Installation

Deformation Noise with Angeliki

2026

Deformation Noise translates Angeliki's contortionist practice into a live acoustic signal. I'm connecting her to neuromuscular sensors and converting her raw contortion stress into feed for a generative sound panel. Using automata principles and rule-based self-evolution, I want a sound synthesis pipeline that can self-organize into a beautiful soundscape.
