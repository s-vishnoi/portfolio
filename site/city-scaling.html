Scaling laws reveal a quiet geometry beneath urban life: as cities grow, patterns emerge in innovation, infrastructure, inequality, and violence. This project uses ideas from physics and complex systems to study how U.S. cities change with population size, and how deviations from expected scaling relationships expose the histories and structures that make each city.
